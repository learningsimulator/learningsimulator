The Human Timelines
Project

Human Timelines is a major and ambitious interdisciplinary effort to bring together, communicate and stimulate research of time series of any kind relevant for understanding our species' cultural evolution and relationship with its physical and ecological environments, from the Paleolithic, to today and into the future.

Contact

For more information about the Human Timeline project, please contact Magnus Enquist (Stockholm University), Peter Søgaard Jørgensen (Stockholm Resilience Center), or Aikaterini Glykou (Stockholm University).

Features

The Human Timelines platform is designed to be a self-contained tool for research and education. Together, we shape the new standard.

Timeline Database

Stores and displays time series, both quantitative and qualitative data as well as events (e.g implementation of regulations and laws).

Timeline Explorer

Provides descriptive, statistical, and numerical methods for analysing and comparing time series including techniques for inferring causal dependencies (e.g. how public opinions influence environmental regulations) and forecasting.

Timeline Builder

Is used for creating new time series from own data (directly entered or from common file formats), from exploration of series in the Timeline Database, or from other databases, and from theoretical work.

Timeline Theory

Contains methods for exploring mathematical models and simulations of temporal processes.

Timeline Publisher

This component is used for making times series publically available (see also user access below) and produce featured time series. Making times series publically available includes a quality assurance procedure to secure the quality of available data, and that necessary information is supplied including where the data was originally published, by whom and other relevant information. Typically it is the author(s) or equivalent that uploads data.

Timeline Communicator

The purpose of Timeline Communicator is to provide easy access to and information about featured time series. It is aimed for users from any part of society (e.g. general public, journalists, policy makers, students and teachers). A featured time serie(s) is based on a topic such as land use, Palaeolithic climate or changes in public opinions about global change, and contains both time series and explanatory text about the topic, methods, conclusions and uncertainties. The time series are typically based on data publicly available from the Timeline Database.
